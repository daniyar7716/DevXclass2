Lexus

Lexus (Japanese レクサス rakususu) (Russian Lexus) is a brand of premium cars produced by the Japanese corporation Toyota Motor. Brand cars are sold in more than 90 countries around the world. Lexus has been present on the Russian market since 2002; as of January 25, 2021, the company has more than 50 dealers in the country.
In 1983, Toyota CEO Eiji Toyoda voiced his desire to create the best car in the world. This is how the project codenamed F1 (Flagship One) was born, the result of which was the Lexus LS 400 - it was from this car that the history of the brand began. F1 engineers and designers designed the Lexus LS 400 with the goal of expanding the international market, while also working on a new V8 engine.
The F1 project was completed in 1989. By this time, 60 designers, 1400 engineers, 2300 technicians and 220 assistants had developed approximately 450 prototypes. The result of all this work was the LS 400. It featured a unique design, a specially developed 4-litre 8-cylinder petrol engine with rear axle drive. The LS 400 was first presented to the public at the North American International Auto Show in Detroit, and official sales began in September 1989.
